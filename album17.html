Review

While I was reviewing, I thought this was going to be among the highest ranked albums yet, but I was surprised to see it didn't even crack 4 stars. This is one of the first albums with intro/interludes so I had to come up with a way to rate them, since technically they count as songs on Spotify. I think both the intro and outro were a great display of her vocals with the old school microphone quality to go along with it and it just scratched my brain in a satisfying way. I heard many of these before since they were released as singles and have had some time to develop into heavy repeats on my playlist, but I was pleased with the quality of the new tracks. So many of the songs were themed around putting an end to relationships that were meant to be over and growing anew while carrying on the lessons learned forward. Langley has the perfect voice for these kinds of tracks and it just goes so well with every kind of background used in this project, both upbeat and faster like in Be Her and slower, longer-kept notes as in Dandelion. This album truly showed her versatility as an artist and I'm excited for some future releases from her. My personal favorites off this album were Be Her, Dandelion, and Loving Life Again.
