Review

It doesn't get better than this. A couple of songs came out prior to the full release and many snippets were released on social media, but I successfully sheltered myself from all of it so I could listen to this whole project all the way through. I also wanted to save the better tracks to be in their prime listening time simultaneously and I don't become sick of it which has been the case for a couple other albums. I was in the car for the first few songs and didn't quite hear the lyrics that well, so my original rankings were a tad different than this 5 star masterpiece you see before you. That being said, each track has something so magical about it that makes it special. For example, the authenticity you feel hearing Heading North and the raw recording from inside the studio, Dan calling back to the first Noah Kahan song I ever heard and all the references to his other lyrics in general, and every single song having a deeper meaning than it seems. My only gripe I have with this album, and clearly it's nothing major as the scores reflect, but I feel at some parts it is so soft and quiet that nothing can be made out and other parts you have to turn your volume down from the sheer noise entering your ears. Overall, this was everything I was hoping for and more, and I'm so glad that so many others enjoy his music as I have. My personal favorites off this album were Doors, Porch Light, and Dan.
