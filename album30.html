Review

I went through a huge Bebe Rexha phase in 2022. I was a fan of the simple lyrics/message and impressive vocals belting out choruses. This feels like a change similar to what Demi Lovato went through and Rexha is not testing out the EDM scene. That isn't the official genre of the album, but it is very clear that many tracks rely on the back track carrying the best parts of the song. I think this change was eventual because of previous partnerships with the likes of David Guetta and other dance music icons. Another gripe I have that I probably would not have noticed unless I was typing out the names of the tracks, but it is the inconsistency of the titles in this album. The DIRTY BLONDE album name is loud and bold and attention catching, almost every song uses standard capitalization on the first letter and then randomly i like you better than me is only lowercase. This may not seem like a big deal, but to me, it shows little thought put into the branding of having one standalone project and more the result of a bunch of different thoughts thrown together. Everything being said, the songs themselves are ~good~ but only in the context of how they could be used. I would not just shuffle this album at a corporate function and expect everyone to get hyped for it, but by myself or in the right party setting, many of these songs can do what they were meant to and bring the energy. I have added a few of these tracks to a running playlist to mostly tune out the lyrics and get a boost from the background tracks. My personal favorites off this album were i like you better than me, Drink And A Little Love, and New Religion. Album was reviewed on June 16th, 2026.
